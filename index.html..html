A Testimony of Fire & Water

3 Years in the Wilderness

Like Elijah by the brook or David in the caves, Prophet Loyel endured three years of homelessness. It was in this desert place that God stripped away the reliance on man and taught him total dependence on the Spirit. Biblically, the wilderness is not a place of abandonment, but of preparation. God brought him through the cold nights and the hunger, refining his spirit for the counseling ministry he leads today.

The Miracle in the Canal

The enemy sought to destroy the call on his life in a horrific event. Prophet Loyel was drugged, causing him to fall unconscious behind the wheel. The car careened out of control, smashing over three roundabouts, tearing through a steel fence, and plunging into a canal.

"In spite of being unconscious, my spirit was awake. I was found still praying."

God's hand sustained him underwater. While the car was destroyed, the Prophet was preserved. This testimony stands as proof that no weapon formed against God's anointed shall prosper.
